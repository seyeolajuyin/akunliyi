PROF. DORA NKEM AKUNYILI

The woman who saved a million lives.


Prof Akunyili,a rare symbol of courage who helped save million of Nigerians from an epidemic of fake drugs.

At a time when fake pharmaceutical drugs were rampant in Nigeria and thousands of Nigerians died daily from the intake of such drug, Prof Akunliyi was called upon to helm the National Food and Drugs Agency and during her tenure there she ensured that production and sales of fake drugs became non-existential.
Here is a timeline of Prof. Akunliyi's life:

  • 1954 - Born in Markurdi, Benue State to to Chief and Mrs. Paul Young Edemobi.
  • 1973 - Enrolled at Queen of Rosary Secondary School, Nsukka,Enugu. She earned several scholarship due her exceptional grades in School Leaving certificate.
  • 1978 - She obtained her B.Sc in Pharmacology from the University of Nigeria, Nsukka. She also starts working as a Hospital Pharmacist at the University Teaching Hospital.
  • 1985 - she obtains her PH.D also from University of Nigeria.
  • 1988 - A riveting year for her. Her Sister, Vivian died from a fake insulin shot. Her fight against counterfeit drugs began
  • 1998 - She begins training sponsored by the World Bank and WHO in Senior Management. This prepared her for future role has the Director General of the National Food and Drug Agency
  • 2001 - She is appointed Director General of NAFDAC.from her very first day in office,she made the total eradication of counterfeit drugs and food a top priority.Before her appointment there was little to no regulation on foods and drugs consumption.Tablets made out of Chalk were been sold at the time. She put a good fight against producers of counterfeit drugs, who made many attempts to scare her with death threats but she was undeterred.
  • 2008 - After six years as the DG of NAFDAC she is appointed Minister of Information and Communications.
  • 2011 - She ventures into politics and runs for the Senate under the AGPA party.She loses to Chris Ngige of ACN.
  • 2014 - After a long fought battle against Uterine Cancer, she dies at the age of 60.

Prof Akunliyi lived a passionate life that was dedicated to protecting lives. In her final public speech shortly before her death, she ended her speech with a this Greek Proverb.

“A society grows great when old men plant trees whose shade they know they shall never sit in.”
